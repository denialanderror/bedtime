One of my earliest memories is of the stories my dad used to invent for my sister and I before bedtime. Instead of reading a book to us, he would ask us both for a character and then create a magical story straight from his imagination.

The age-old tradition of storytelling is an important part of a child's social and mental upbringing but many parents today feel they lack the time, confidence or imagination to tell bedtime stories to their children.

Bedtime was created to allow parents to participate in their child's nightly routine by telling engaging stories in a modern way.
